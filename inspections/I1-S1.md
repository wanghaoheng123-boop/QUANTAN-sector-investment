### Spot-check #3 — R3 F3.3 (GEX averages call gamma + put gamma at same strike)

**Independent verification:**
1. Read `lib/options/gex.ts:47-58, 66-72`. Confirmed `entry.gammaSum += gamma` accumulates across both call and put sides; line 68 averages.
2. Theoretical check: For European options with identical (S, K, T, r, σ), gamma is type-independent (basic BSM property, Hull 2017 p404). **However**, since `chain.ts` enriches each contract with its OWN IV, call IV ≠ put IV at the same strike under skew. Reviewer's claim is correct — averaging gammas computed from different IVs underweights the dominant side.
3. Cross-reference: dealer-positioning convention in Krishnan (2017) confirms the `(callOI - putOI) × gamma` formulation but leaves "which gamma" ambiguous; Squeezemetrics whitepaper (cited) explicitly uses each-side's-own-gamma.

**Citation check:** Krishnan (2017) Ch.6 — verified ISBN, page-cite plausible. Squeezemetrics whitepaper — known industry reference.

**Verdict:** R3 F3.3 — SUSTAINED with reviewer's caveat noted (the issue is real, severity could be downgraded if `chain.ts` already enforces single-IV-per-strike; reviewer flagged this self-dissent).

## Independent re-derivation (math)

I independently hand-computed Sortino for a 100-bar test series (60 positive, 40 negative, mean = +0.0005, MAR = 0):
- Negative deviations: 40 values
- Sum of squared negative deviations: ≈ 0.018 (synthetic)
- N-1 denominator (current `indicators.ts` impl): variance = 0.018 / 99 ≈ 0.0001818, dsd = 0.01349
- n_d denominator (`engine.ts` impl): variance = 0.018 / 40 = 0.00045, dsd = 0.02121
- Ratio: 0.02121 / 0.01349 = 1.572
- Predicted by F2.1: sqrt(N / n_d) = sqrt(100 / 40) = sqrt(2.5) ≈ 1.581

Match within 0.6%. **R2 F2.1 quantitative claim sustained.**
